Tourism & Attractions in Roxas

Discover natural wonders, cultural heritage, and the gateway to Boracay and nearby islands.

Welcome to Roxas Tourism

Roxas is a premier tourist destination and strategic gateway to world-famous Boracay Island. With beautiful beaches, natural caves, eco-forests, cultural sites, and excellent port facilities, Roxas offers diverse experiences for every traveler. From adventure seekers to cultural enthusiasts, discover the best of Oriental Mindoro.

Gateway to Boracay & RORO Ferry Services

Port of Roxas: Your Boracay Connection

The Port of Roxas in Barangay Dangay is the main transit hub to Boracay Island. RORO (Roll-On/Roll-Off) ferries operate regular routes from Roxas to Caticlan, Malay, Aklan, with a scenic 4-hour journey across the Tablas Strait. From Caticlan, it's just a short boat ride to Boracay.

Ferry Information

Operating Companies: Starlite Ferries, Montenegro Shipping Lines, and other local operators provide comfortable passenger and vehicle transport. Frequency: Regular daily and weekly schedules for convenient travel planning. Fares: Affordable rates make Roxas an economical gateway compared to other routes to Boracay.

Beyond Boracay

The port also connects passengers to Romblon (Odiongan and Sibuyan Island) and Palawan. Roxas serves as a strategic transit point for island-hopping adventures throughout Mimaropa region.

Top Tourist Attractions in Roxas

San Rafael Cave & Environs

One of the most visited attractions in Roxas, San Rafael Cave features stunning limestone formations and underground passages. Guided cave tours introduce visitors to stalactites, stalagmites, and the geological history of the region. The surrounding area offers scenic views and picnic spots. Perfect for adventure seekers and nature photographers.

San Vicente Eco-Forest & River Trail

Immerse yourself in lush forest trails along pristine rivers. San Vicente Eco-Forest offers peaceful hiking opportunities through native vegetation, offering a cooler escape from coastal heat. Visitors can enjoy swimming in natural pools and observing local flora and fauna.

Roxas Heritage Park

Located in the town center, Roxas Heritage Park is a well-maintained public space featuring monuments celebrating municipal history, sculptures of historical figures, and landscaped gardens. It's an ideal spot for photos, peaceful walks, and learning about local heritage.

Beaches Around Roxas

The Tablas Strait beaches offer beautiful coastal scenery with gray sand, clear waters perfect for swimming, and scenic sunsets. Several barangays feature beach access: Dangay, Cantil, Victoria, and San Rafael offer quiet beach experiences for swimming, boating, and fishing.

Buhid Mangyan Cultural Village & Communities

Experience authentic indigenous culture by visiting Buhid Mangyan communities. Learn about traditional crafts, weaving, agriculture, and way of life. Respectful cultural tourism supports local artisans. Purchase authentic handicrafts and products directly from the community.

Fishing Village Tours & Maritime Heritage

Visit working fishing villages to witness authentic maritime culture. Observe fishermen preparing boats, learn about traditional fishing methods, and enjoy fresh seafood at local restaurants. Early morning visits offer the best experience of fishing activity.

Water Sports & Marine Activities

Enjoy boating, island hopping tours, diving in selected areas, and fishing excursions. Several tour operators arrange water-based activities exploring nearby islands and marine sanctuaries.

Roxas Fiestas & Local Events

SIPAG Festival (January 25)

The annual SIPAG Festival celebrates the municipality's main livelihood: Saging (Bananas), Isda (Fish), Palay (Rice), Prutas (Fruits), and Gulay (Vegetables). The festival features colorful parades, cultural performances, trade fairs showcasing local products, street food, and community activities. It's the perfect time to experience Roxas culture and support local businesses.

Founding Day (November 15)

Roxas celebrates its founding anniversary with community activities, cultural shows, and thanksgiving celebrations.

Religious & Community Celebrations

Throughout the year, various barangays host fiestas honoring their patron saints. These include parades, masses, processions, and social gatherings. Local events offer great opportunities to experience genuine Filipino hospitality and traditions.

Roxas Lifestyle & Culture

Maritime Heritage & Fishing Culture

Fishing is central to Roxas identity. The municipality's economy and daily life revolve around the sea. Visitors can experience authentic fishing traditions by visiting working harbors, learning about local fishing methods, and enjoying fresh seafood caught daily by local fishermen.

Agricultural Heritage

The upland barangays showcase diverse agriculture: rice paddies in Cantil, Victoria, Dangay, Odiong, and San Isidro; fruit orchards in Little Tanauan and San Miguel; and backyard livestock farming in Maraska, San Jose, and San Rafael. Visitors can explore these agricultural areas and learn about sustainable farming practices.

Filipino Hospitality & Bayanihan Spirit

The residents of Roxas embody Filipino warmth and community cooperation (bayanihan). Visitors often experience genuine hospitality, welcoming locals, and the relaxed but vibrant pace of small-town life. Community celebrations include all, reflecting the spirit of unity that defines the municipality.

Local Arts & Crafts

Artisans in Roxas produce quality handicrafts including woven items, pottery, and traditional crafts. Support local artisans by purchasing authentic products at Heritage Park markets and during the SIPAG Festival.
