About Roxas

Roxas is a municipality in Oriental Mindoro, Philippines, founded in 1948. Located 126 kilometers from Calapan, it serves as a major port and gateway to nearby islands including Boracay, Romblon, and Palawan.

With 20 barangays and a population of over 58,000 residents, Roxas is known for its fishing industry, agricultural products, and cultural heritage. The municipality continues to grow as a transit hub and tourism destination in Mimaropa region.

Why Visit Roxas?

Gateway to Boracay

The main port for RORO ferries to Boracay, making travel convenient and affordable for island hoppers.

Natural Attractions

San Rafael Cave, San Vicente Eco-Forest, pristine beaches, and Roxas Heritage Park await visitors.

Fishing Industry Hub

Experience authentic fishing traditions along Tablas Strait with excellent seafood and maritime heritage.

Growing Economy

Discover business opportunities in agriculture, shipping, and tourism with modern port facilities.
