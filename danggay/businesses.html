Business & Economic Opportunities

Discover growing companies, industries, and investment opportunities in Roxas.

Business & Economy Overview

Roxas is a dynamic economic center with diverse sectors including international shipping, fishing, agriculture, port services, and tourism. The Port of Roxas serves as a crucial economic hub connecting Oriental Mindoro to Boracay, Romblon, and Palawan islands. The municipality is classified as a 2nd municipal income class with 20 barangays supporting various livelihoods and business opportunities.

With modern port infrastructure, growing tourism, and agricultural exports, Roxas offers promising business opportunities for entrepreneurs and investors seeking to expand in a strategic location.

Major Shipping & Maritime Companies

Starlite Ferries International

Business Type: Passenger and Vehicle Ferry Service
Routes: Roxas ↔ Caticlan/Boracay, Roxas ↔ Romblon
Service Areas: Port of Roxas, Barangay Dangay
Specialty: RORO (Roll-On/Roll-Off) ferries providing reliable transportation for tourists and residents. Comfortable onboard facilities, dining services, and vehicle transport capabilities.

Economic Impact: Major employer, driver of tourism revenue, key infrastructure partner.

Montenegro Shipping Lines

Business Type: Cargo and Passenger Shipping
Routes: Roxas to Boracay and inter-island destinations
Service Areas: Port of Roxas
Specialty: Efficient cargo logistics for local products, equipment, and passenger services. Supports both tourism and commercial trade.

Economic Impact: Facilitates export of local agricultural and fishing products to wider markets.

Port of Roxas Authority

Business Type: Port Management and Operations
Location: Barangay Dangay
Specialty: Modern port facilities, cargo handling, vessel services, customs clearance, and maritime safety operations. Handles both passenger terminals and freight operations.

Economic Impact: Core infrastructure supporting regional trade, tourism transport, and economic development.

Fishing & Seafood Processing Companies

Business Type: Fishing, Seafood Processing, Export
Products: Fresh fish, shrimp, canned seafood, processed fish products
Locations: Multiple fishing companies operate throughout Roxas barangays
Specialty: Sustainable fishing practices, cold storage facilities, quality processing for domestic and international markets. Support for small-scale fishermen and fishing cooperatives.

Economic Impact: Primary industry employer, export revenue generator, food security provider.

Investment Opportunities in Roxas

Strategic Advantages

  • Port Infrastructure: Modern facilities with proven capacity and operational efficiency
  • Gateway Location: Primary route to Boracay with 4-hour ferry service and steady passenger flow
  • Growing Tourism: Increasing visitor numbers supporting hotels, restaurants, and activity services
  • Agricultural Products: Quality export potential for tropical fruits, fishery products, and organic goods
  • Labor Availability: Skilled workforce in maritime, fishing, agriculture, and service sectors
  • Government Support: Municipal classification enabling business incentives and development programs

Business Support & Development

Municipal Government Business Services

The Municipal Government of Roxas supports business development through licensing assistance, regulatory guidance, and business-friendly policies. The municipality generated ₱213 million revenue in 2020 and positioned second municipal income class showing strong economic performance.

Banking & Financial Services

Traditional banks and microfinance institutions provide credit and financial services supporting business operations, agricultural financing, and personal enterprises.

Cooperatives & Business Organizations

Farming cooperatives, fishing associations, merchant groups, and trade organizations support members through collective action, market access, and shared resources.

Skills & Workforce Development

Technical schools and community colleges (Roxas Institute of Science & Technology, Clarendon College, Southern Mindoro Maritime School) provide workforce training in maritime, agriculture, and service sectors.

Summary of Economic Sectors

Secondary Industries: Food processing, port operations, cargo handling
Service Sector: Tourism, hospitality, retail, transportation, professional services
Emerging Growth Areas: Tourism infrastructure, digital services, renewable energy, value-added agricultural products
