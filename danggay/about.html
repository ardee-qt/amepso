About Roxas, Oriental Mindoro

Discover our rich heritage, vision for development, and the municipality's role as a gateway to the islands.

Our History

Founded on November 15, 1948, Roxas was created as an independent municipality from Mansalay through Executive Order No. 181 by President Elpidio Quirino. It was named after President Manuel Acuña Roxas, who passed away on April 15, 1948, making Roxas the first town named in his honor.

Originally known as Paclasan, the municipality comprises 20 barangays across a land area of 8,526 hectares. Located 126 kilometers from Calapan, Roxas developed as a major port due to its strategic position along the Tablas Strait.

In 2003, the Strong Republic Nautical Highway (SNRH) was inaugurated, opening the Port of Roxas in Barangay Dangay. This transformed Roxas into a transit hub connecting to Boracay, Romblon, and Palawan, bolstering tourism and international trade.

The Municipality of Roxas

Roxas is home to over 58,000 residents (2024 census) distributed across 20 barangays. The municipality is characterized by strong maritime traditions, agricultural heritage, and a growing service economy centered on the Port of Roxas.

Key Facts:

  • Founded: November 15, 1948 as an independent municipality
  • Population: 58,428 (2024 census)
  • Land Area: 8,526 hectares (85.26 km²)
  • Barangays: 20 barangays including Dangay, San Rafael, San Vicente, Victoria, and more
  • Primary Industries: Fishing, agriculture (palay, bananas, coconuts, rambutan, lansones), and port services
  • Strategic Location: 126 km from Calapan; gateway port to Boracay (4-hour ferry), Romblon, and Palawan
  • Language: Filipino (Tagalog), Romblomanon
  • ZIP Code: 5212

20 Barangays of Roxas:

Bagumbayan (Poblacion), Cantil, Dangay, Happy Valley, Libertad, Libtong, Little Tanauan, Mabuhay, Maraska, Odiong, Paclasan (Poblacion), San Aquilino, San Isidro, San Jose, San Mariano, San Miguel, San Rafael, San Vicente, Uyao, and Victoria.

The municipality values sustainable development, environmental stewardship, and inclusive growth. Through cooperative efforts and modern port infrastructure, Roxas continues to strengthen its role as a regional economic center and prime tourist destination.
